I am a behavioral and decision scientist who tackles challenges by taking an experimental, quantitative approach to answering questions and developing solutions.

My work is particularly shaped by insights from behavioral economics, psychological science, and data science. Though my research interests are broad, the focus of my academic work was on the intersection of emotion and decision-making processes.

Currently, I am an Associate at BEworks where I help solve real-world problems related to financial decision-making, sustainability, and healthcare. Before this, I completed my PhD in social psychology with a minor in quantitative psychology at The Ohio State University and then was a postdoctoral fellow in decision neuroscience at University of Toronto.

I use a variety of methods in my work: behavioral experiments, computational modeling, eye-tracking, physiological measures, neuro-imaging, and pharmacological interventions.
